# OpenSteps

` You'll find in this project two folders: 1. Wireframe 2. Hi-fi `

## Vision

OpenSteps is the Wikipedia of real-world procedures — an open, community-driven platform where anyone can find clear, verified, step-by-step instructions for navigating bureaucratic processes. From registering a business to getting a driver’s license, OpenSteps makes the invisible visible.

## Mission

To eliminate information asymmetry in civic processes by building the world’s most reliable, community-verified database of procedures — starting in Sierra Leone, scaling across Africa, and eventually globally.

## Principles

- Open knowledge — All information is free and publicly accessible
- Community verification — Truth emerges from collective validation, not central authority
- Evidence-based trust — Real receipts, real forms, real proof
- Radical transparency — Every edit, every review, every change is visible
- Local first, global ambition — Deep accuracy in one country before breadth

## The Problem

Across Sierra Leone and much of Africa, critical everyday information is:
Hidden — locked in the heads of clerks, lawyers, and “people who know people”
Scattered — spread across multiple offices, WhatsApp groups, and word-of-mouth
Outdated — fees change, offices move, requirements shift without notice
Intentionally opaque — information asymmetry creates opportunities for rent-seeking
This affects everyone: the entrepreneur who spends three days learning a process that should take thirty minutes; the diaspora investor who can’t navigate remote bureaucracy; the student who doesn’t know the steps to apply for a scholarship. The cost is not just time and money — it’s opportunity lost at scale.

### Who Feels This Pain
Audience        Pain Point
Citizens        Waste hours/days navigating unclear processes; vulnerable to exploitation
Entrepreneurs   Business registration, licensing, and compliance are black boxes
Diaspora        Cannot navigate home-country bureaucracy remotely
NGOs            Lack structured data on governance and service delivery
Researchers     No reliable public dataset on how government processes actually work


## The Solution
- OpenSteps is an open, mobile-first web platform where:
- Anyone can submit a step-by-step guide for a real-world process
- The community verifies accuracy through a structured review workflow
- Contributors upload evidence (receipts, forms, photos) to build trust
- Editors fast-track or curate content for quality
- Every guide carries a “Last Verified” date and full revision history
- The result: a living, breathing, community-maintained knowledge base that makes bureaucracy transparent.

### What a Guide Contains
- Overview box: estimated time, cost, difficulty, office location, last verified date
- Step-by-step instructions with numbered actions
- Required documents list
- Fees breakdown (official fees, known unofficial costs)
- Office locations and hours
- Processing time (official vs. realistic)
- Evidence uploads: photos of receipts, forms, offices
- Community tips from people who’ve completed the process
- Comments and discussion
